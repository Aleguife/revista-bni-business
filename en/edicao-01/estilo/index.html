Where passion meets purpose:
the story of someone who
found their place

Anderson spent 25 years balancing the corporate world with his true passion: jewelry. It was at BNI that he discovered how to turn an attentive eye into a business of impact

By Fernanda Sodré

Some people are born with a different way of seeing. A gaze that perceives beauty where others see only metal and stone. A gaze that recognizes stories in every shine, meaning in every shape, possibility in every material.

Anderson has always had that eye for jewelry. From a young age, he felt a magnetic attraction to that universe where art meets precision, where beauty meets value, where life's important moments take tangible form.

But for years, that gaze was only half of his life.

Years divided — until he found someone who believed alongside him

On one side, the corporate world. A solid career, steps climbed, experiences accumulated. On the other, jewelry — not as a hobby, but as a calling he could not ignore.

It was a life that was technically functional but emotionally exhausting. Half of him in each world. Never whole in either of them.

He built deep knowledge about gems and design. He developed relationships with suppliers and artisans. He won over clients who trusted that singular eye. Even so, the body does not lie. Corporate stress intensified. Jewelry began to ask for more — more dedication, more presence, more life.

That was when Anderson met someone who helped him change everything. She saw in him the potential to turn that divided passion into full dedication.

"Lea encouraged me," Anderson says. "More than that — she decided to dive in with me. 

They were not starting from scratch — they were giving full attention to what they had always built in the margins. Now they were two attentive gazes, four hands working, two voices believing.

The years of full dedication brought growth. Anderson became known among those who understand that a jewel is not just an ornament — it is a story, a milestone, an emotional and financial investment.

Three years that amplified thirty

Three years ago, Anderson joined BNI. And everything he had been building found the growth that was missing.

"BNI elevated the awareness of my work to the right people. Those who understand that a jewel is not just shine, it is meaning."

More of the right clients began to appear. Those who value quality over quantity. Who seek relationships, not transactions. Who become ambassadors because they genuinely trust the work.

It was this consistent growth that made the next step possible.

The partnership born of trust

In 2025, Anderson met Silvia Ramos, also from BNI Salto, and together they turned a wish into a company in Organa.

They shared the same vision: combining decades of experience with the courage to do luxury in a completely different way. Organa was not just a name change — it was a declaration of purpose. The materialization of thirty years of experience to reinvent how Brazilians access true luxury.

How Organa reinvents access to luxury

In-house workshop: excellence that is never outsourced

The complete structure: laser welding for invisible repairs, in-house gold and rhodium plating, master goldsmith with over 30 years of experience who has worked in renowned jewelry houses.

"Total control over quality, deadlines and confidentiality," Anderson explains. "No one outside our team touches your piece. It is security from start to finish."

Exclusive service: the jeweler comes to you

Your jewelry comes to your home. You choose in the privacy of your own space, with all the time you need, involving whoever you wish in the decision.

They offer something rare: absolute discretion. No travel, no foot traffic, no public exposure. It is exclusive, private service in the comfort of your own space. Physical, emotional and financial security.

Strategic auctions: accessible luxury across Brazil

He travels the country taking part in auctions to assemble an exclusive ready-to-deliver collection. He serves clients all over Brazil with authentic pieces from Tiffany, Cartier, Bvlgari, H.Stern at prices well below the market.

"We mine unique opportunities," Anderson explains. "We have crafted wedding bands in the same H.Stern model for half the price, with twice the weight of the brand-name piece. True luxury without traveling abroad or paying boutique prices."

Bespoke creation

Exclusive pieces from scratch. Every jewel is a collaborative project: from concept to drawing, from stone selection to finishing. With an in-house workshop and master goldsmith, everything is carried out in absolute confidentiality.

You materialize a dream, you create something unique in the world. Literally: only one piece like it exists, and it is yours.

Redesigning heirlooms

Jewelry kept for decades takes on new life. With laser welding, new settings and gold or rhodium plating, the piece emerges with a brand-new finish — carrying the soul of the old jewel.

"We transform a brooch into a necklace, a large ring into earrings, forgotten pieces into something contemporary," Anderson details. "It is keeping memories alive in a renewed form."

The shine of the right choice

What makes Organa shine is not just gold or diamonds. It is the journey behind it.

It is the courage to dive in whole. It is finding someone who believes and dives in with you. It is building for thirty years and finding the right network to amplify your purpose. It is the partnership born of genuine relationships.

Today, Anderson sees more than a company. He sees years of dedication taking new form. He sees an eye for jewelry he always had finally receiving the stage it deserves. He sees possibilities that only open up when you stop dividing yourself and start dedicating yourself fully.

It is authenticity. It is commitment. It is the ability to look at something and see not only what it is, but what it can become.
